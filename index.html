The Fasolati Therapeutic Platform

Three product lines. Three mechanisms. One unified architecture. A surgeon‑engineered system that treats the mouth as a controllable origin node of systemic inflammation.

LyticaPrecision Ecological Rescue

From Greek lysis — targeted dissolution. Hunt and eliminate. Spare everything else.

A two‑stage tray‑applied biofilm intervention system. Stage 1 (BREACH) uses hydrogen peroxide effervescence plus enzymatic matrix degradation — DNase I, Dispersin B, Proteinase K — to collapse biofilm architecture in 5–10 minutes. Stage 2 (HUNT) deploys bacteriophages targeting P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, and A. actinomycetemcomitans, plus recombinant endolysins and lactoferrin, delivered in a dual‑weight hyaluronic acid matrix for sustained contact and tissue support.

Stage 1 — Breach

H₂O₂ effervescence disrupts biofilm oxygen gradients. DNase I degrades extracellular DNA scaffold. Dispersin B hydrolyzes PNAG adhesin matrix. Proteinase K cleaves surface-bound adhesion proteins. Combined: 4.5 log colony reduction.

Stage 2 — Hunt

Species-specific bacteriophages bind exclusively to bacterial surface receptors. Recombinant endolysins degrade peptidoglycan from outside. Lactoferrin sequesters iron from pathogen metabolism. HA carrier ensures 4+ hours mucosal residence time.

Clinical Targets

Active periodontal disease — stages III and IV
Peri-implantitis — biofilm on titanium surfaces
Post-surgical infection control
Refractory cases non-responsive to SRP + antibiotics
LithosStructural Regeneration

From Greek lithos — stone. What teeth are meant to be. Rebuild, don't just protect.

Built on Professor Alvaro Mata's elastin‑like recombinamer (ELR) technology from the University of Nottingham, published in Nature Communications (November 2025). Lithos drives epitaxial hydroxyapatite crystal growth in native enamel orientation — not amorphous mineral deposition, but organized crystallographic reconstruction from the lattice up.

The Mata Technology

Elastin-like recombinamers provide a protein scaffold that templates hydroxyapatite nucleation at the demineralized surface. Epitaxial growth — crystals form in the same orientation as native enamel prisms. Not a coating. True structural integration at the molecular level.

Fasolati Integration

Mata's ELR is the mineralization engine. Fasolati's dual-weight HA carrier is the delivery and mucosal integration layer. Lithos combines both: mineralization scaffold + bio-adhesive matrix for complete tooth reconstruction.

Clinical Targets

Early caries — rebuild, don't fill
Erosive tooth wear — acid damage reversal
Post-whitening sensitivity — restore demineralized surface
Hypomineralization (MIH) — developmental enamel defects
Post-orthodontic white spot lesions
LoriaMolecular Purification

From Latin luere — to purify. The original Fasolati formulation. Physical barrier, heal, hydrate, and hold.

Loria replaces chlorhexidine's indiscriminate kill with intelligent ecological remodeling. Biofilm‑dispersing enzymes dismantle the extracellular polysaccharide scaffold. Dual‑weight hyaluronic acid creates a physical barrier while driving fibroblast proliferation by 35% within 7 days. Castor oil reduces IL‑1β and TNF‑α while reinforcing pellicle lipid barriers. Bio‑adhesive film formation via pullulan and PVP ensures hours of therapeutic residence.

Barrier + Heal

High MW HA forms protective scaffolding on mucosal surface. Low MW HA penetrates deeper tissue, stimulates fibroblast proliferation, drives re-epithelialization (8/9 wounds healed in 24 hours vs 0/9 saline).

Hydrate + Hold

Dual-weight HA retains moisture at mucosal surfaces. Pullulan + PVP create cross-linked adhesion network for extended therapeutic residence. CPC at 0.05% provides selective antimicrobial baseline.

Clinical Targets

Daily oral ecology maintenance — chlorhexidine replacement
Biofilm management — prevent recolonization after treatment
Post-surgical mucosal healing support
Xerostomia (dry mouth) — sustained hydration

Inside the Black Box

You deserve to know exactly how the AI that manages your health actually works — what it knows, where it learned it, how it checks itself, and how it gets smarter the longer you use it.

01 — The Brain

What Powers SciAgent

Not a chatbot. A clinical reasoning engine trained on the science of inflammation.

SciAgent is built on a large language model — the same class of AI behind the most advanced reasoning systems in the world — but fine-tuned specifically for oral-systemic health. Think of the base model as a medical school graduate: broad knowledge, strong reasoning. Our fine-tuning is the residency — years of specialized training in the exact pathways that connect your mouth, your gut, and your systemic inflammation.

Foundation Model

A state-of-the-art language model provides the reasoning backbone — the ability to read your data, recognize patterns, weigh tradeoffs, and explain its thinking in plain language. We don't build this from scratch. We build on the best.

Domain Fine-Tuning

We then train the model on oral-systemic health specifically: periodontal pathology, gut barrier dynamics, inflammatory cascades, biomarker interpretation, pharmacology of our product lines. It doesn't guess about inflammation. It reasons from mechanism.

Constrained Outputs

SciAgent can only recommend interventions from the Fasolati product ecosystem. It cannot prescribe medications, diagnose diseases, or replace your doctor. It operates within strict clinical guardrails — what to rinse, swallow, and avoid. Not medical advice. Protocol guidance.

Explainable Reasoning

Every recommendation SciAgent makes comes with a "why." Not a black box output — a transparent chain of reasoning you can read, question, and share with your clinician. If it suggests increasing your Loria frequency, it tells you exactly which data point triggered that change.

02 — The Education

Where It Learned What It Knows

Trained on peer-reviewed science. Not internet health forums. Not sponsored content.

The quality of an AI is only as good as the quality of its training data. SciAgent's knowledge base is built from curated, peer-reviewed clinical literature — the same sources your periodontist and gastroenterologist rely on. We don't scrape the internet. We curate the science.

Clinical Literature

Thousands of peer-reviewed papers on periodontitis, gut permeability, inflammatory biomarkers, microbiome ecology, and the oral-systemic connection. Sources include The Lancet, Nature, Journal of Clinical Periodontology, Gut, and specialized inflammation research.

Biomarker Databases

Reference ranges, population distributions, and clinical significance thresholds for hs-CRP, HbA1c, zonulin, calprotectin, LPS-binding protein, and other inflammatory markers. SciAgent knows what your numbers mean in context — not just whether they're "high" or "low."

Product Pharmacology

Deep knowledge of every Fasolati formulation — mechanisms, dosing, interactions, contraindications, expected response curves. When SciAgent adjusts your Lytica protocol, it understands the enzymatic and phage mechanisms at the molecular level.

Clinical Practice Guidelines

Current treatment guidelines from the American Academy of Periodontology, American Gastroenterological Association, and the European Federation of Periodontology. SciAgent's recommendations align with — never contradict — established standards of care.

What SciAgent is never trained on: social media health claims, supplement marketing, anecdotal testimonials, or unreviewed preprints. If it's not in the peer-reviewed literature or validated clinical databases, SciAgent doesn't know it — and that's by design.

03 — The Fact-Check

How It Verifies Everything

Every recommendation runs through three layers of verification before it reaches you.

We built SciAgent with a fundamental assumption: AI makes mistakes. The question isn't whether errors happen — it's whether the system catches them before they reach you. Our answer is a three-layer verification architecture that treats every output as unproven until confirmed.

Layer 1

Evidence Match
Every claim is cross-referenced against its source literature. If SciAgent says "hs-CRP above 3.0 correlates with increased cardiovascular risk," it must cite the specific studies.

→

Layer 2

Clinical Guardrails
Rule-based safety checks catch anything outside established parameters. Dosing limits, contraindication flags, interaction warnings — hard-coded, not learned. The AI cannot override these.

→

Layer 3

Confidence Scoring
Every recommendation carries a confidence score. High confidence: act on it. Medium: SciAgent explains the uncertainty. Low: it flags the question for your clinician instead of guessing.

⊘

Never Invents

SciAgent cannot fabricate studies or statistics. It retrieves from its verified knowledge base or says "I don't know."

⟐

Never Overreaches

If your data suggests something outside its scope — a possible diagnosis, a medication question — it routes you to your doctor, not a guess.

↻

Always Auditable

Every recommendation is logged with its reasoning chain. Your clinician can review exactly why SciAgent made each suggestion.

04 — Your Model

How It Learns You

The longer you use Fasolati, the smarter your personal model becomes.

SciAgent starts with population-level science — what works for most people. But you're not most people. Over weeks and months, it builds a model of your specific inflammatory patterns, your response curves, your habits, and your triggers. This is where it transforms from a general tool into your personal health engine.

Week 1 — Baseline

SciAgent ingests your initial data: lab results, oral exam findings, any wearable data you connect. It establishes your personal baseline across the three vectors — Oral Ecology, Gut Integrity, Systemic Inflammation — and sets starting protocols based on population evidence.

Weeks 2–4 — Response Mapping

As you use the products and log data, SciAgent begins tracking how your body responds. How quickly does your hs-CRP drop after a Lytica intervention? Does your sleep quality correlate with gut protocol adherence? It's building your personal response curves.

Months 2–3 — Pattern Recognition

With enough data, SciAgent starts identifying your unique patterns. Maybe your inflammation spikes every time you travel. Maybe your oral ecology dips on weekends. Maybe Biolumen works faster for you than population averages predict. These insights shape increasingly precise protocols.

Month 4+ — Predictive Protocols

This is where the model matures. SciAgent begins anticipating inflammatory events before they show up in your biomarkers — adjusting protocols proactively based on behavioral patterns, seasonal trends, and your established response curves. It moves from reactive to predictive.

Ongoing — Continuous Refinement

Your model never stops learning. Every new lab result, every oral exam, every week of wearable data sharpens the picture. And as the broader Fasolati user base grows, anonymized population insights feed back into the system — your model benefits from the collective without ever exposing your data.

Turning an Unlit Corner On

Fasolati started with a simple observation in an operating room: the mouth and the body have never been treated as one connected system. We're changing that.

The Origin

From the OR to the Operating System

For nearly two decades, Dr. Thad Connely has operated at the intersection of the mouth and the body — as an oral and maxillofacial surgeon performing total joint reconstructions, treating the most severe manifestations of what happens when oral disease goes systemic.

The pattern was always the same: patients arriving with advanced disease that started as inflammation nobody monitored. Periodontitis that seeded systemic infection. Gut dysbiosis that accelerated joint destruction. Biomarkers that were never checked until the damage was done.

The insight wasn't that these systems were connected — the literature has shown that for decades. The insight was that nobody had built the infrastructure to treat them as one system. No product line spanned both origin nodes. No AI correlated the data across domains. No protocol existed that told a patient what to rinse, swallow, and avoid — today, based on their specific inflammatory profile.

Fasolati is that infrastructure. Built by a surgeon who got tired of operating on problems that should have been intercepted upstream.

The Mission

Make Inflammation Visible, Controllable, and Preventable

Three out of five people die of chronic inflammatory diseases. The mouth and the gut are the two origin nodes where that inflammation begins — and the two places where it can actually be intercepted before it becomes cardiovascular disease, neurodegeneration, metabolic syndrome, or autoimmune destruction.

Treat the Network, Not the Symptom

The body is a connected system. Oral health, gut health, and systemic inflammation are not three separate problems — they're one network. Fasolati is built to treat the network.

Protocols Over Advice

Vague wellness recommendations fail. Specific, daily, data-driven protocols succeed. SciAgent doesn't suggest you "eat better" — it tells you exactly what to do today based on your data.

Surgeon-Grade Science, Patient-Grade Access

The same mechanisms used in the operating room — enzymatic biofilm disruption, phage therapy, HA tissue engineering — delivered as daily consumer products anyone can use.

Transparency as Architecture

Every recommendation comes with a "why." Every data point is yours. Every algorithm is auditable. We built "Inside the Black Box" because trust isn't a feature — it's the foundation.

Core Stats
3 of 5 deaths from chronic inflammation+
Chronic inflammatory diseases — cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes, Alzheimer's, autoimmune disorders — account for 3 in 5 deaths globally. This isn't a comorbidity. It's the primary mechanism. Fasolati treats inflammation not as a symptom to suppress but as a network failure with identifiable origin nodes: the mouth and the gut.
34.6% with elevated hs-CRP+
High-sensitivity C-reactive protein is the gold-standard serum marker for systemic inflammation. Over a third of US adults have elevated levels — most unaware. The Fasolati Engine uses hs-CRP as a primary input in its Three-Vector Score, correlating it with oral ecology and gut integrity data for real-time inflammatory load tracking.
90% of spend downstream of inflammation+
The healthcare system spends 90% of its $4.1 trillion annual budget treating downstream consequences of chronic inflammation. Almost nothing is spent on interception at origin. Fasolati's thesis: move upstream. Target the controllable nodes where inflammation begins, at a fraction of the cost of treating where it ends.
Key Concepts
Chronic inflammation as primary death mechanism+
The paradigm shift. Inflammation isn't a side effect of disease — it IS the disease mechanism. From atherosclerotic plaque formation to tumor microenvironment signaling to neuroinflammatory cascades in Alzheimer's, the same mediators (IL-6, TNF-α, CRP) drive the pathology. Fasolati treats the body as a network where inflammation propagates from origin nodes. Control the nodes, control the network.
hs-CRP as early warning+
hs-CRP is produced by the liver in response to IL-6 signaling — a systemic alarm bell. SciAgent AI integrates hs-CRP trends with oral biomarker data (pocket depth, bleeding on probing) and gut markers (zonulin, calprotectin) to detect inflammatory acceleration early — when intervention is still cheap and effective.
Mouth and Gut as controllable origin nodes+
The mouth harbors 700+ bacterial species in biofilm communities millimeters from the bloodstream. The gut epithelium is a single cell layer separating trillions of microbes from systemic circulation. Fasolati's architecture — Lytica, Lithos, Loria for the mouth; Magisnat, Biolumen, Monch Monch for the gut — targets the two highest-leverage intervention points in human health.
Pathophysiology
Periodontitis as systemic disease+
Periodontitis isn't a dental problem — it's a systemic inflammatory disease with oral manifestations. The ulcerated pocket epithelium exposes roughly 72 cm² of wound surface to 500+ bacterial species. P. gingivalis, T. denticola, and A. actinomycetemcomitans have been found in atherosclerotic plaques, synovial fluid, amniotic fluid, and Alzheimer's brain tissue.
Biofilm entry via ulcerated epithelium+
Dental biofilm is a structured polymicrobial community protected by an extracellular matrix resisting both immune response and conventional antimicrobials. When the gingival epithelium ulcerates, biofilm organisms gain direct hematogenous access. Lytica™ addresses this with enzymatic matrix disruption followed by targeted bacteriophages that eliminate keystone pathogens without disrupting commensal ecology.
Pathogen link to Brain, Heart, and Placenta+
P. gingivalis in Alzheimer's brain tissue — gingipain proteases driving tau phosphorylation. F. nucleatum in colorectal tumors and placental tissue, linked to preterm birth. T. denticola in atherosclerotic plaques. These aren't correlations — established mechanistic pathways. Eliminate the oral reservoir, cut off the systemic seeding.

Pathophysiology
Barrier failure and LPS translocation+
The gut epithelium — one cell thick — separates the largest microbial community on earth from systemic circulation. When tight junctions fail, LPS from gram-negative bacteria translocates into the bloodstream, triggering TLR4-mediated NF-κB activation. Metabolic endotoxemia drives insulin resistance, fatty liver disease, and cardiovascular inflammation.
Metabolic and neuro disease drivers+
Gut-derived LPS drives neuroinflammation via vagal and hematogenous routes — depression, anxiety, neurodegeneration. SCFA deficiency impairs blood-brain barrier integrity and microglial homeostasis. Gut permeability drives insulin resistance independent of caloric intake. Biolumen and Magisnat target these specific pathways.
Oral-gut microbiome link+
1.5 liters of saliva swallowed daily — carrying oral microbes into the GI tract. With PPI use or high oral pathogen loads, F. nucleatum and P. gingivalis colonize the gut, driving intestinal inflammation. Fasolati treats this as one connected system: oral intervention reduces pathogenic load reaching the gut.

02 — Origin Node One

The Mouth

The periodontal pocket is not a local nuisance; it is a vascularized wound that leaks inflammatory signals and pathogens into systemic circulation, liver, endothelium, placenta, and brain.

Periodontitis behaves as a systemic inflammatory disease with local manifestations.
Oral pathogens like P. gingivalis are found in atherosclerotic plaques, brain tissue, and placental tissue.
Pocket epithelium is thin, ulcerated, and bathed in high‑load biofilm—an engineered portal of entry.

The Oral Node: Therapeutics Platform

03 — Origin Node Two

The Gut

The intestinal barrier is a dynamic border. When it fails—through diet, dysbiosis, or oral pathogen seeding—lipopolysaccharides and inflammatory mediators leak into circulation, driving hepatic inflammation, insulin resistance, and neurodegeneration.

Oral dysbiosis and swallowed biofilm reshape the gut microbiome.
LPS translocation is a direct driver of hepatic and vascular inflammation.
Peripheral inflammation crosses the blood–brain barrier and accelerates neurodegenerative trajectories.

The Gut Node: Barrier and Metabolism

Mediterranean nutraceuticals, prebiotic fibers, and metabolic snacks built to restore barrier integrity and microbiome diversity, not just macronutrients.

Magisnat: Mediterranean‑pattern nutraceuticals tuned for anti‑inflammatory signaling.
Biolumen: engineered prebiotic fibers to feed the right taxa and dilute dietary error.
Monch Monch: metabolic snacks as behaviorally‑realistic interventions instead of discipline theater.
